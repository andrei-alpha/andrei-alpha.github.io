Problem Planks

The fence of Britain's top Chemical Engineering Factory has proved to be unsecure. Thus, the management has decided to fix it by contracting two unskilled workers. Arrived at the scene, the workers found that the fence is made up of N boards (numbered 1, 2, 3 ... N from left to right). Moreover, each board is of different height. Following, they determined that the first thing to do is to remove the N boards. Thus, over the next N days they remove the smallest board. As the workers progress with their work, they realize something rather bizarre. If in day T they removed the plank from position X, the height of all planks to the left of X increased with T units.

Requirement

Find the difference between the height of the removed planks and the height of the initial planks.

Input

The first line of the input contains N. Then N lines follow, on line i is the initial height of plank i-1.

Restrictions

  • 1 ≤ N ≤ 100.000
  • 1 ≤ Xi ≤ 1.000, Xi is the initial height of plank i
  • If they have to choose from more planks of equal height, the workers will choose the left most one
  • The first day is day 1
  • Plank i (i ≥ 2) has in it`s left planks 1, 2, 3 ... i-1

    • Output

    The first line of output should contain the required number K.

    Sample Input

    7 
9 
10
6
6
4
9
8

    Sample Output

    36
